What is Bitcoin?

Bitcoin is a decentralized digital currency, without a central bank or single administrator, that can be sent from user to user on the peer-to-peer bitcoin network without the need for intermediaries.

Bitcoin transactions are verified by network nodes through cryptography and recorded in a public distributed ledger called a blockchain.

Key Features of Bitcoin:

  • Decentralized
  • Peer-to-peer transactions
  • Limited supply (21 million BTC)
  • Open source and transparent

FAQs:

What is a Bitcoin wallet?

A Bitcoin wallet is a software program where Bitcoins are stored. Technically, Bitcoins are not stored anywhere; there is a private key (secret number) for every Bitcoin address that is saved in the Bitcoin wallet of the person who owns the balance.

Is Bitcoin legal?

The legality of Bitcoin varies from country to country. Some countries fully embrace Bitcoin, while others impose restrictions or outright bans. It’s important to check the regulations in your country.

How can I buy Bitcoin?

You can buy Bitcoin on various cryptocurrency exchanges, through peer-to-peer platforms, or using Bitcoin ATMs. You’ll need a Bitcoin wallet to store your Bitcoins.

What are Bitcoin miners?

Bitcoin miners use specialized computers to solve complex mathematical problems that verify and add Bitcoin transactions to the blockchain. Miners are rewarded with newly minted Bitcoins and transaction fees.
